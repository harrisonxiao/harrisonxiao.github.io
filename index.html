About

Hello! My name is Harry (Youkang) Xiao (肖友康). I am a senior at Brandeis University majoring in Psychology and Economics, with a minor in Women’s, Gender, and Sexuality Studies. I will graduate in May 2025 and begin my doctoral studies (Ph.D.) in School Psychology at the University of California, Berkeley under the mentorship of Dr. Frank C. Worrell.

My research interests broadly center on identity development, the risk and resilience factors in shaping students’ mental health and academic outcomes, and the evaluation of culturally responsive interventions that promote equitable learning outcomes and healthy developmental trajectories—particularly among historically marginalized student populations.

Please feel free to reach out if you'd like to connect or collaborate!

Research

2025 — Beyond the Scores 2.0: Identity, Resilience, and Standardized Testing

Building on the original Beyond the Scores project, this study investigates how socioeconomic status shapes minoritized college students’ access to standardized test preparation and its psychological and academic consequences. Integrating qualitative interviews with multiple regression and structural equation modeling, we examine how resilience and ethnic-racial identity mediate the effects of socioeconomic disadvantage on psychological distress and academic engagement. Findings aim to inform culturally responsive, trauma-informed interventions that promote positive developmental outcomes for multiply marginalized students.

2024 — Beyond the Scores 1.0: Mental Health and Academic Engagement in Standardized Testing

This mixed-methods project explores the mental health and academic engagement of students of color in the context of standardized testing. Combining validated survey scales with in-depth interviews, we examined the emotional, psychological, and financial stressors associated with test preparation, as well as perceptions of test fairness. This work highlights the need for more equitable assessment practices and student-centered education policy reforms.

2022–2023 — Racism on Social Media: A TikTok Case Study

This study investigates the intersection of race, gender, and online harm by analyzing how marginalized youth experience racism on TikTok. Using a Python-based detection model, we identified patterns of hate speech, slurs, and microaggressions across 1,000 videos. Qualitative interviews further explored students’ experiences with digital harassment, perceptions of algorithmic bias, and coping strategies. Findings emphasize the psychological toll of online racism and the urgent need for equity-driven content moderation policies.
